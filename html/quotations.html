Tags used under quotation HTML elements are (blockquote, q , abbr, address, cite, and bdo).

a.) blockquote

Here is a quote from WWF's website:

For 50 years, WWF has been protecting the future of nature.The world's leading conservation organization,WWF works in 100 countries and is supported by1.2 million members in the United States and close to 5 million globally.

b.) q

WWF's goal is to: Build a future where people live in harmony with nature.

c.) abbr

The HTML (abbr tag) defines an abbreviation or an acronym, like "HTML", "CSS", "Mr.", "Dr.", "ASAP", "ATM"

The WHO was founded in 1948.

d.) address

The HTML (address tag) defines the contact information for the author/owner of a document or an article.

The text in the (address element) usually renders in italic, and browsers will always add a line break before and after the (address element).

Written by John Doe.
Visit us at:
Example.com
Box 564, Disneyland
USA

e.) cite

The HTML tag defines the title of a creative work (e.g. a book, a poem, a song, a movie, a painting, a sculpture, etc.).

f.) bdo

This is to reverse the text means read in bi-directional way

This text will be written from right to left
